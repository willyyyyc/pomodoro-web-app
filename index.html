The Pomodoro technique is a simple time management strategy that recommends fixed periods of deep focus (traditionally 25 minutes) followed by short periods of rest (usually 5 minutes is sufficient). These steps are repeated 4 times (each interval is called a pomodoro— inspired by the tomato-shaped kitchen timer the creator Francesco Cirillo used) before a longer (15 minute) break is taken. The process is repeated until the task is completed.
